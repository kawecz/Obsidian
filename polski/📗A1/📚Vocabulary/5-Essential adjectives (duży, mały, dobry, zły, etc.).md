### 🏷 **Essential Adjectives in Polish (Podstawowe przymiotniki)**

#### 📌 **Introduction**

Adjectives in Polish describe **qualities, characteristics, and states** of nouns. They must **agree in gender, number, and case** with the noun they modify. This means adjectives **change their endings** depending on whether the noun is masculine, feminine, or neuter.

In this lesson, we will cover the most **commonly used adjectives**, their meanings, forms, and example sentences.

## 🏆 **2. Adjective Endings in the Nominative Case**

### 🔹 **Singular Forms**

|🏷 **Gender**|🔚 **Ending**|📝 **Example**|
|---|---|---|
|**Masculine**|-y / -i|_duży kot_ (_big cat_), _ładny dzień_ (_nice day_)|
|**Feminine**|-a|_mała książka_ (_small book_), _dobra kawa_ (_good coffee_)|
|**Neuter**|-e|_nowe auto_ (_new car_), _ciepłe mleko_ (_warm milk_)|

💡 **Rule:**

- **Soft-stem adjectives** (ending in _-k, -g, -ch_) take _-i_ instead of _-y_ in masculine form (e.g., _wysoki → wysoki chłopak_ but _miękki → miękki dywan_).
    

---

### 🔹 **Plural Forms**

|🏷 **Gender**|🔚 **Ending**|📝 **Example**|
|---|---|---|
|**Masculine Personal** (people)|-i / -y|_mądrzy ludzie_ (_wise people_), _wysocy mężczyźni_ (_tall men_)|
|**Non-Masculine (fem. & neut.)**|-e|_ładne domy_ (_nice houses_), _nowe książki_ (_new books_)|

💡 **Note:**

- Masculine personal plural adjectives use _-i_ instead of _-y_ (**mądrzy ludzie**, not ❌ _mądrzy kobiety_).

## 🚀 **3. Adjectives in Sentences**

🔹 **Describing Objects:**

- _To jest duży dom._ (_This is a big house._)
    
- _Mam nową torbę._ (_I have a new bag._)
    
- _To jest drogie auto._ (_This is an expensive car._)
    

🔹 **Describing People:**

- _On jest wysoki._ (_He is tall._)
    
- _Ona jest miła._ (_She is nice._)
    
- _Dzieci są szczęśliwe._ (_The children are happy._)
    

🔹 **Describing Feelings and States:**

- _Jestem smutny._ (_I am sad._ – said by a man)
    
- _Jestem zmęczona._ (_I am tired._ – said by a woman)
    
- _Jest ciepło na dworze._ (_It is warm outside._)
    

---

## 🏁 **Conclusion**

Polish adjectives are **essential for describing people, objects, and situations**. Learning their **endings and agreement rules** will help you form natural and grammatically correct sentences.

🎯 **Key Takeaways:**  
✅ Adjectives must **match** the noun in gender, number, and case.  
✅ **Masculine adjectives** usually end in _-y_ or _-i_, **feminine in -a**, and **neuter in -e**.  
✅ **Plural forms** differ for **masculine personal** and **non-masculine nouns**.

💡 **Tip:** Practice by describing things around you using different adjectives!
