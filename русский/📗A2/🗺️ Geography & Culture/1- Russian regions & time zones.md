### üß† 3 - Time Zones in Russia (–ß–∞—Å–æ–≤—ã–µ –ø–æ—è—Å–∞ –†–æ—Å—Å–∏–∏)

Russia spans 11 time zones, stretching from UTC+2 to UTC+12. This means that when it is noon in Moscow, it could already be midnight the next day in the far eastern city of Kamchatka. The time zones are structured to reflect Russia's vast geographical size.

- **Moscow Time (–ú–æ—Å–∫–æ–≤—Å–∫–æ–µ –≤—Ä–µ–º—è, MSK)** ‚Äî The time zone of Moscow and the westernmost regions of Russia, including Saint Petersburg, is UTC+3.
    
- **Time Zones of Siberia and the Far East** ‚Äî Moving eastward, time zones in Russia increase by one hour for every region. Some notable time zones include:
    
    - **Yekaterinburg Time (UTC+5)** ‚Äî Includes regions like Sverdlovsk Oblast and Tyumen.
        
    - **Irkutsk Time (UTC+8)** ‚Äî Includes Irkutsk and surrounding areas.
        
    - **Vladivostok Time (UTC+10)** ‚Äî Covers the Russian Far East, including the city of Vladivostok.
        
- **Chukchi Time (UTC+12)** ‚Äî The easternmost time zone, which covers the Chukchi Autonomous Okrug and the Kamchatka Peninsula.

- **Media and Entertainment** ‚Äî Television broadcasts and news coverage often have different schedules depending on the region, taking into account the time differences.
