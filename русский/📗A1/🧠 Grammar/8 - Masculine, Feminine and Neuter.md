In Russian, all nouns belong to one of three grammatical genders: **Masculine**, **Feminine**, or **Neuter**. Understanding gender is essential because it affects how adjectives, pronouns, and verbs agree with nouns.

## üß† **Quick Rules Summary**

- **Most nouns ending in a consonant ‚Üí Masculine.**
    
- **Nouns ending in -–∞/-—è ‚Üí Feminine.**
    
- **Nouns ending in -–æ/-–µ ‚Üí Neuter.**
    
- **Nouns ending in -—å ‚Üí Masculine or Feminine (must be memorized).**
    
- **Nouns ending in -–º—è ‚Üí Neuter (always).**
